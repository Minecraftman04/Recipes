King Edward Deep-Fried Chips

Fluffy in the middle, crisp and golden outside. This uses a lower-temperature first fry to cook the potato through, then a hotter second fry for crunch.

Serves
2–3
Prep time
25 mins
Cook time
20–25 mins
Best potato
King Edward
Deep fryer safety: dry the chips very well before frying, never fill oil above the max line, and do not leave hot oil unattended.

Ingredients

  • 700 g King Edward potatoes, peeled or unpeeled
  • Vegetable oil, sunflower oil, or beef dripping, enough for your fryer
  • Fine sea salt, to finish
  • Optional: malt vinegar, ketchup, mayonnaise, or chip-shop curry sauce

Method

  1. Cut the potatoes into chips around 1–1.5 cm thick. Try to keep them similar in size so they cook evenly.
  2. Rinse the chips in cold water until the water is less cloudy. Soak for 20–30 minutes if you have time.
  3. Drain, then dry the chips extremely well with a clean tea towel or kitchen paper. Wet chips can make hot oil spit.
  4. Heat the deep fat fryer to 140°C. Fry the chips in small batches for 8–10 minutes, until they are softened and pale, not browned.
  5. Lift the basket, drain well, and spread the chips on a tray. Let them steam-dry and cool for at least 10 minutes. For even better chips, cool them fully.
  6. Increase the fryer to 180°C. Fry the chips again for 3–5 minutes, until golden and crisp.
  7. Drain, tip into a bowl, season immediately with salt, and serve hot.

Tips

  • Do not overcrowd the basket. Too many chips at once cools the oil and makes the chips greasy.
  • For chunkier chip-shop-style chips, cut closer to 1.5 cm and add a little extra time to the first fry.
  • If the chips brown too quickly on the second fry, drop the final temperature slightly to 170–175°C.
  • King Edward potatoes are floury, so they are good for fluffy interiors. Handle them gently after the first fry so they do not break up.
