Quick King Edward Deep-Fried Chips

A faster version of the two-stage chip method. The chips go straight from the first cook into the second cook as soon as the oil has heated back up.

Serves
2–3
Prep time
15 mins
Cook time
15–20 mins
Best potato
King Edward
Deep fryer safety: dry the chips very well before frying, never fill oil above the max line, and do not leave hot oil unattended.

Ingredients

  • 700 g King Edward potatoes, peeled or unpeeled
  • Vegetable oil, sunflower oil, or beef dripping, enough for your fryer
  • Fine sea salt, to finish
  • Optional: malt vinegar, ketchup, mayonnaise, or chip-shop curry sauce

Method

  1. Cut the potatoes into chips around 1–1.5 cm thick. Try to keep them similar in size so they cook evenly.
  2. Rinse the chips in cold water until the water is less cloudy, then drain them straight away.
  3. Dry the chips extremely well with a clean tea towel or kitchen paper. Wet chips can make hot oil spit and will stop them crisping properly.
  4. Heat the deep fat fryer to 140°C. Fry the chips in small batches for 8–10 minutes, until softened and pale, not browned.
  5. Lift the basket and let the chips drain while the fryer heats to 180°C. Do not add a separate rest or cooling step.
  6. As soon as the oil reaches 180°C, fry the chips again for 3–5 minutes, until golden and crisp.
  7. Drain, tip into a bowl, season immediately with salt, and serve hot.

Tips

  • This is quicker than the full recipe, but the chips may be slightly less crisp because they do not get a proper cooling period.
  • Do not overcrowd the basket. Too many chips at once cools the oil and makes the chips greasy.
  • If the chips brown too quickly on the second fry, drop the final temperature slightly to 170–175°C.
  • For the quickest result, start heating the fryer while you cut and rinse the potatoes, then dry the chips thoroughly before frying.
