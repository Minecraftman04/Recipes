Miller & Carter–Style Beef Dripping Steak Sauce

A rich, glossy and intensely beefy sauce inspired by the beef dripping sauce served with steak at Miller & Carter. It is closer to a concentrated jus than a thick gravy.

Makes
4 small portions
Prep time
10 mins
Cook time
30–35 mins
Best served with
Steak and chips
Copycat recipe: this is a homemade approximation inspired by the restaurant sauce, not Miller & Carter's official recipe.

Ingredients

  • 50 g beef dripping
  • 100 g fatty stewing beef, such as chuck, skirt or brisket, thinly sliced
  • 2 shallots, finely diced
  • 3 garlic cloves, finely chopped
  • ½ star anise
  • 80 ml dry red wine
  • 20 ml ruby port
  • 500 ml strong, good-quality beef stock
  • ½ teaspoon brown or white sugar
  • A few drops of red wine vinegar, to taste
  • Fine salt and freshly ground black pepper, to taste
  • ¼ teaspoon xanthan gum
  • Optional alternative to xanthan gum: 1 teaspoon cornflour mixed with 1 tablespoon cold water

Method

  1. Heat a wide saucepan over a medium-high heat and add roughly one-third of the beef dripping.
  2. Add the beef, shallots, garlic and star anise. Fry for 5–6 minutes, stirring regularly, until the beef and shallots are deeply browned. Do not rush this stage, as the browning creates much of the steakhouse flavour.
  3. Remove and discard the star anise. Pour in the red wine and port, then scrape the browned pieces from the bottom of the pan.
  4. Boil until the wine mixture has reduced to roughly one-third of its original volume.
  5. Add the beef stock and remaining dripping. Bring to the boil, then simmer uncovered for 20–30 minutes, until reduced by about half and intensely beefy.
  6. Strain the sauce through a fine sieve into a clean saucepan. Press the solids gently to extract the liquid, but do not force them through the sieve.
  7. Add the sugar and black pepper. Taste before adding salt, as concentrated stock may already be salty. Add the red wine vinegar two or three drops at a time until the sauce tastes rich but not greasy.
  8. Place over a low heat and gradually whisk in the xanthan gum. Use it sparingly, as it continues to thicken for a minute. When using the cornflour alternative, whisk in the slurry and simmer for 1–2 minutes.
  9. Stir in any resting juices from the cooked steaks, taste for seasoning, and serve hot.

Tips

  • Use a concentrated or gelatin-rich beef stock. A weak stock cube will produce a much less intense sauce.
  • The finished sauce should be glossy and pourable, rather than as thick as conventional gravy.
  • For an alcohol-free version, replace the wine and port with extra beef stock plus 1 teaspoon of balsamic vinegar. The flavour will be different but still rich.
  • Keep refrigerated for up to 3 days, or freeze in individual portions. Reheat gently and whisk before serving.
  • For a gluten-free sauce, check that the beef stock and all other packaged ingredients are certified gluten-free, and use xanthan gum rather than cornflour if preferred.
